Year End Reflection (2016)

10 Questions to Ask Myself

1. What am I the proudest of this year?

Realized improvement areas as a professional.
Persevered with learning and self-motivating.
Became clearer who deserves time and resource to maintain friendship with.
Lived a life in my own way - kept exercise and running, had hair cut shorter.

2. Am I passionate about my career?

Yes. The more you learn, the less you know.

3. What lessons have I learned?

Before deciding to believe a person, thoroughly think about three criteria:
(1) Once upon a time, he or she talked with you about a thing, crying or feeling painful.
(2) Never is he or she late, leaves early, break an appointment at last minute.
(3) He or she never speaks ill of others when being with you.
source:https://www.zhihu.com/question/36343659

4. Where am I feeling stuck?

Still passionate about supply chain, but no definite career direction.
Spent time and money on coffee and Japanese but achieving no significant progress.

5. Where do I need to allow myself grace?

Left current employer and position with no remarkable career achievement.

6. Who do I need to forgive? What should I let go?

Forgive those who made me unhappy - forgiving means forgetting and never mentioning any more.
Admit I am a person who has interests in too many things - always give up halfway instead of carrying through to the end.

7. What old habits would I like to release? What new habits would I like to cultivate?

Healthy eating habits - more vegetables, less carbohydrate.
Manage personal finances and start a relationship.
Create career development plan based on this year-end reflection and follow it strictly.

8. How well did I take care of my body, mind, and soul?

Took care of my body - kept exercise and completed the program of Running 100 Days.
Did not pay special attention on mind and soul growth.
Always said it was the favorite book but still half done with reading - The Road Less Traveled.

9. What do my finances look like?

Saved some money but not enough to afford expensive or luxury things - no house, no car - since graduation.
Supported parents case by case but not regularly.
Most spending was on physiological needs instead of self-actualization.

10. How did I spend my free time?

Took two months to learn HTML by myself and published personal website: www.akajc.cn.
Spent spare time on Coursera/Lynda/Wechat and learnt about database/programming/office applications etc.
Systematically studied project management and successfully passed PMP certification with satisfactory result.
Benefiting from above, found a desirable job in Beijing.
Bought and read some books about coffee and Japanese.
Knew some new friends and traveled once (Huangshan).
